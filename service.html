Deep tissue massage is used to relax the muscles of the human body. It is an advanced massage technique in which the masseur applies deep pressure to rearrange the connective tissue and muscle layers.

It is very effective for the treatment of chronic pain (stiff neck, back pain, recovery after injury, repetitive strain, carpal tunnel syndrome, posture problems, gluteal muscles, upper back muscle tension, osteoarthritis pain, sciatica, piriformis syndrome, tennis elbow, fibromyalgia, etc.).

The masseurs use their hands, forearms, elbows and knuckles to move slowly over the stiff area and apply pressure to reach the sublayers of the muscles to relieve the pain.

During deep tissue massage, you need to keep your muscles fully relaxed to achieve a better treatment effect. Although the affected area may be a little painful during treatment, the pain will disappear after a period of time. Some people need 2 or 3 days to completely recover from muscle pain after the massage. It is recommended that you drink plenty of water afterwards, which will help eliminate toxic substances in the body.
